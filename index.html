CLIMATE CHANGE & GLOBAL WARMING

Its real. And its happening.

Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth's climate system observed since the pre-industrial period due to human activities. Climate change not only involves changes in Earth's temperature, but it also encompasses extreme weather events, rising sea levels, endangering wildlife populations and many other grave effects (NRDC,2016). Our Earth's condition worsens year by year


Effects of Global Warming

As we can see, there has been many wildfire events from the years 2008 to 2018. Wildfires are just one example of extreme events caused by climate change. Wildfires are a result of changes in climate which include warmer and drier weather. The United States of America is no stranger to wildfires as nearly 72000 wildfires occur on average per year.

A temperature anomaly is the difference from an average or baseline temperature. As we can observe, the global temperature anomaly has increased over the years.

The global ocean temperatures have been rising ever since the year 1977. Because of the rising amounts of greenhouse gases, most of the atmospheric heat is passed back into the ocean. Water covers more than 70% of the Earth's surface. Due to this, it can absorb large amounts of heat. If the ocean absorbs more heat than it releases, its heat content increases.

Causes of Global Warming

Carbon dioxide (Co2) is the primary greenhouse gas emitted through human activities.

When it comes to fighting global warming, trees have emerged as one of the most popular weapons.Forests help stabilise the climate. They regulate ecosystems, protect biodiversity, play an integral part in the carbon cycle, support livelihoods, and can help drive sustainable growth.To maximise the climate benefits of forests, we must keep more forest landscapes intact, manage them more sustainably, and restore more of those landscapes which we have lost.
